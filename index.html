A DEFINITION, QUICKLY

"Generative," in one line.

A recording of a wind chime is the same every time. The wind chime itself never repeats. Generative music builds instruments that are wind chimes, not recordings of them.

Arranged track

The recording

  • Every note decided in advance
  • Identical on loop 1 and loop 100
  • "Live" = pressing play, mixing faders
Generative system

The wind chime

  • Rules + a source of chance, every bar
  • Never repeats identically, even at the same tempo
  • Live = steering probability, in real time

Four building blocks, hands on the machine

The recursive four-layer MIDI engine, the Markov-driven bassline, the stochastic drum sampler, and the raga-weighted lead — each demoed live, each preceded by a question the room has to guess before I show them.

The hook

Four generative layers can slide into noise. A single fixed motif is the anchor a listener's ear needs to feel the piece as music, not chaos — the discipline inside the freedom.
